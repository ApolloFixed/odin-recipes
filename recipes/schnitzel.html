Schnitzel

Description:

A schnitzel is a breaded and fried cutlet of meat, typically made with pork, chicken, veal, beef, or turkey. The dish originated in Austria and Germany, where it is a popular comfort food. A traditional schnitzel is made by pounding the meat thinly, coating it with flour, eggs, and breadcrumbs, and then frying it in oil until crispy and golden brown.

Ingredients needed:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Lemon wedges for serving (optional)

Steps:

  1. Pound the chicken: Use a meat mallet or a rolling pin to pound the chicken breasts to an even thickness of about 1/4 inch.
  2. Season the chicken: In a shallow dish, mix together the flour, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Dredge the pounded chicken breasts in the flour mixture, shaking off any excess.
  3. Dip in eggs: Dip the flour-coated chicken breasts in the beaten eggs, making sure they are fully coated.
  4. Bread the chicken: Roll the egg-coated chicken breasts in the breadcrumbs, pressing the crumbs gently onto the meat to ensure they stick.
  5. Fry the schnitzel: Heat about 1/2 inch of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Fry the breaded chicken breasts for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.
  6. Serve: Serve the schnitzel hot with lemon wedges on the side. Squeeze a bit of lemon juice over the top of the schnitzel for added flavor.
